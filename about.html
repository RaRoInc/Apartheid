Apartheid

During 1959 and 1960, violence broke out in several South African cities. In March 1960 police fired upon a crowd protesting against the pass laws in Sharpeville, killing at least 69 people and injuring many others. During 1985, south africans started to rebel against apartheid. They wanted to be considered human and have human rights like the white people. In their fight for human rights, Protests and violence were used to fight for their rights along with economic sanctions, International outrage, the ANC and Nelson Mandela. We will be focusing on The protests and violence that happened in S.A.
